A 2D procedural stealth game

During my 2020/2021 university year, I chose to lead my own team of developers and artists to make a small game as part of the UBC Game Development club. The game we made was Parvenu, a top-down 2D procedurally generated stealth game where the aim for the player was to collect the blue print in the level, as well as money which was planned to be used to purchase upgrades. Each level would be dotted with guards that have a cone of vision, chasing the player if they enter their sight. The levels were randomly generated and pieced together to provide a fresh experience with each playthrough.

As the project leader and lead developer, I had to communicate and collaborate with my other team members to assign coding tasks, direct the art style, and help formulate a mood and theme for the music. To organize our game's production, we used Github to maintain a version history and track changes.
